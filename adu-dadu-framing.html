ADU & DADU FAQ

ADU & DADU Framing Questions, Answered

What is the difference between an ADU and a DADU?

An ADU (accessory dwelling unit) is a secondary living unit attached to or inside the main house — like a basement apartment or attached in-law suite. A DADU (detached accessory dwelling unit), often called a backyard cottage, is a stand-alone structure on the same lot. Seattle allows up to two accessory units per residential lot, and we frame both types.

How long does it take to frame a DADU in Seattle?

Most DADUs and backyard cottages are framed in 2 to 4 weeks depending on size, complexity, and weather. A typical 800–1,000 sq ft single-story DADU frames faster than a two-story unit with a complex roof line. We provide a framing schedule with every bid so your project timeline stays predictable.

Do you work with homeowners or only general contractors?

Both. Most of our ADU and DADU framing work comes through general contractors and ADU builders, but we also frame for owner-builders who are managing their own permit and acting as their own GC. Either way, you get a detailed written bid within 48 hours.

How much does ADU framing cost in Seattle?

Framing cost depends on square footage, number of stories, roof complexity, and structural requirements like shear walls and engineered beams. Rather than quoting a generic per-square-foot number, we review your actual plan set and return a detailed line-item bid within 48 hours — free, with no obligation. For background on framing costs, see our guide: How Much Does Framing Cost in Seattle?

Do you frame garage conversions and home additions too?

Yes. In addition to new ADU and DADU structures, we frame garage-to-ADU conversions, home additions, second-story additions, and structural remodels throughout King and Snohomish County.
